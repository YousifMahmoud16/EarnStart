How to Make Money Online If You're Under 18 (Legally and Safely)

If you’re a teen or younger than 18 and want to make money online, you might feel stuck.

Many websites require you to be 18+, have a bank account, or show a national ID. But don’t worry — there are real and legal ways to earn money online even if you're underage.

In this guide, I’ll show you how to earn online under 18 using platforms that actually work in 2025 — no scams, no fake promises.

1. Start a Niche Blog (Just Like This One)

Yes, teens can blog too!

  • A free Blogger or WordPress account
  • A topic you love (gaming, fitness, finance, tech, school life...)
  • Simple writing skills

Earn from:

  • Google AdSense (you may need help from a parent)
  • Affiliate marketing
  • Selling digital products like eBooks or guides

2. Join Affiliate Programs That Don’t Require Age Verification

Many affiliate platforms like Amazon require 18+, but some don’t. Examples include:

  • ShareASale
  • ClickBank
  • Digistore24
  • Impact (some programs allow teens)

Promote your links on:

  • Blog
  • Instagram
  • TikTok
  • YouTube
  • Reddit (in niche communities)

3. Offer Freelance Services on Fiverr (13+ Allowed!)

Fiverr allows people as young as 13 years old to join (with permission).

Services you can offer:

  • Logo design (use Canva)
  • Transcription
  • Writing articles
  • Proofreading
  • Social media post design

Tip: Start cheap ($5–$10) to get your first reviews fast.

4. Use Microwork Sites (Some Allow Under 18)

Websites like:

  • Clickworker
  • SproutGigs
  • RapidWorkers

Sometimes allow under 18s if you don’t withdraw to a bank. Use PayPal or crypto if allowed in your country.

Tasks include:

  • Testing websites
  • Categorizing images
  • Simple surveys
